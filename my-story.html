I applied to 300 jobs and got 1 interview

Not exaggerating.

After being laid off twice since 2022, I was doing what everyone tells you to do:

  • Fix your resume
  • Apply everywhere
  • Tailor every application

I did that for 6 weeks.

I got one interview.

That interview didn't come from a resume

It came from a comment.

I replied to a post from someone talking about hiring.

They responded the same day.

We talked the next day.

A week later I was speaking with the hiring manager.

That was my only interview.

That's when something clicked

Applying wasn't working.

Conversations were.

And once you see it, you can't unsee it

Think about it:

Every hiring manager is already on LinkedIn.

They're posting. Commenting. Talking.

Meanwhile everyone else is:

uploading resumes into a black hole

The problem

This sounds obvious in hindsight, but actually doing it sucks.

You have to:

  • Find the right posts
  • Know what to say
  • Not sound like a bot
  • Do it consistently

I tried doing it manually.

It doesn't scale.

So I built something for myself

Not as a startup idea.

Just because I needed it.

Something that:

  • Finds relevant posts
  • Writes thoughtful comments
  • Actually sounds like a human

That turned into Junior.

What it actually does (no fluff)

  • Finds posts from people hiring or talking about roles
  • Generates comments that start conversations
  • Helps you get replies instead of being ignored

That's it.

No resume blasting.

No spam DMs.

What happened after

Instead of this:

apply → wait → nothing

It became:

comment → reply → conversation

And that's where opportunities actually come from.

If you're skeptical, that's fair

I was too.

But I also know what it feels like to:

  • Apply to everything
  • Hear nothing back
  • Wonder what you're doing wrong

This was the only thing that worked for me.
